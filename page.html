Oh, The Places You'll Go

I've been very priviledged in my life to travel to over twenty countries. For the past seven years I had the wonderful priviledge of living in the UK. Along with learning how to land a sarcastic zinger better than a local, I also found a love for football (or what we call stateside, soccer), learned how to make a proper cup of tea, and made friends from the world over. If you couldn't tell already, I didn't want to leave, but atlas, that is life. The beauty that is the countryside in England is arguably unmatched. I spent most of the seven years wondering in forests, getting lost kayaking lochs in Scotland, and putting back a pint with the best of them. England is and will always be my home away from home and the selected family I had to leave behind will always be with me in my heart. I'm grateful for the adventures I was able to go on, the friends who became family, and all the experiences that helped me become the woman I am today.

Funny story, that is too long to include in the fun fact bit, I started my sleeve on my left arm before I knew I had to move back to the states. At this point I have a sleeve that lining is nearly all the way done, and shading that's half-way done for the lining. I sat three, seven hour days, back-to-back, to try and get it done before I came home, but alas, I will have to return in early 2023 to sit another three, seven hour days, back-to-back to complete it. Don't cry for me, I have a higher pain tolerance than most and was very lucky to snag appointments with my artist. I'm thinking of getting my right arm fully sleeved, but I'll wait until my left is done before I try and find someone. I may hold off though because my two to five year plan is to move back to Europe.

Why I Want To Be A Software Engineer

There are several different reasons on why I want to become a software engineer. Let's start off with the practical and obvious: job secuity and the salary you can earn in comparison to cost of living. As someone who used to work in tech in England, I understand the pressures that can be placed on you, but also know the benefit of the salary that you are compensationed with for that stress. I think the saying goes, "In this economy...," if you have the ability to learn and be good at it, why wouldn't you?!

On a less practical, we live in a capitalist death-trap, money makes the world go round society, I like problem solving. I also believe in working with your strengths and being someone who's brain likes to brake down things to the smallest steps to find out why and how things work, I felt that software engineering would be a great fit to feed that part of my brain. I also like to spot the smallest of errors, it's something that my brain does well, and I am hopeful will lend to my strengths in the back-end. I have a brother and several friends who are a software engineers and they've been telling me for years now, that I just needed to bite the bullet and do it, so here I am. Finally believing in my potential the way those around me do.

Have friends and family already in the industry has also helped to set realistic expectation of the stress load of the job. There is a reason the perks look as good as they do. Have this well-balaced out look going into the course, I think will only lend itself to me. I'm ready to work my butt off and change my life for the better on the otherside. One last reason I want to become an engineer is becuase of the doors and opportunies it will open for me.
